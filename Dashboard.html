TT
T-Test Insight

Subscribers are slightly older (40.9 vs 39.9 yrs). Statistically significant but Cohen's d=0.083 indicates a small practical effect. Age alone is insufficient to differentiate subscribers.

AN
ANOVA Insight

Call duration differs by job type (F=3.09, p<0.001). Students and retired customers have longer calls. Technicians have significantly shorter durations vs blue-collar, retired & student groups.

χ²
Chi-Square Insight

Marital status is significantly associated with subscription (χ²=122.7). Single customers subscribe at 14%, notably higher than married (10.2%) or divorced (10.3%).

PC
PCA Insight

PC1 captures macroeconomic context (euribor3m, emp.var.rate, nr.employed). PC2 captures prior campaign history (pdays). 6 components needed to explain 90% of total variance.

KM
K-Means Insight

3 clear customer segments with silhouette=0.766. Cluster 3 (1,705 customers) is the high-potential premium segment. Cluster 2 is the largest volume segment for broad targeting.

LD
LDA Insight

90.91% accuracy, F1=0.546. Class imbalance limits recall (48.5%). emp.var.rate, cons.price.idx, and contact type are the strongest LD1 discriminators — consistent with logistic regression.

Key Recommendations

① Target Prior-Success Customers
64.3% sub rate for prior-success vs 7.3% for prior-failure. Build a dedicated recall list.
② Quality Calls Over Quantity
Longer duration = higher conversion. Train agents to deepen conversations. Cap contacts at 3–4 per customer.
③ Prefer Cellular Channel
Cellular contact doubles subscription odds vs telephone. Prioritise cellular outreach.
④ Target Tertiary-Educated
16.47% sub rate for tertiary vs 5.23% for primary education. Tailor financial messaging accordingly.
